About Us

We are a cat and dog friendly animal shelter. Cats and dogs of all ages and breeds are welcome. Two different options are offered to our customers: adopting or fostering. Fosters provide temporary care for an animal while adopters provide permanent care.

Mission Statement

We thrive to provide loving homes to animals in need. Some of our main values include compassion, dedication, empathy, love, and integrity. Our mission is to enrich the lives of both the animals and people throughout our community. We seek to prevent pet homelessness, promote the health of the animals, and develop a human-animal connection. Our main goal is to provide a home for every one of our animals.

INTAKE

We intake the following types of cats and dogs


  • Strays

  • Schuck-Stripling Animal Center warmly welcomes stray cats and dogs. If you have found a stray, please bring it to one of our locations during operating hours.

  • Lost

  • If you have found lost a pet, please bring the animal to one of our locations. We will care for the animal until it is either claimed by the owner or 3 months pass. After this time, with no response, we will allow the animal to be either fostered or adopted. We recommend checking our shelters every other day if you have your lost pet. We also recommend reaching out to local vets to see if your pet was brought there. Contact all other shelters. *Please be aware fees may apply to reclaim your lost pet due to mandated laws.

  • Surrendered

  • Before giving up your pet, please consider other options. Getting rid of your pet should be the last resort. An appointment is required to surrender your animal. Our appointments are usually booked two weeks out.

Adoption Information

Adopting an animal is always a special event. It is our job to make sure that every animal finds a safe and loving home. In order to adopt an animal you must meet and agree to certain terms and conditions.

  • You must be 18 years of age or older.
  • If you live in an apartment, you must have paid any additional security fees and you must be willing to pay any additional rent.
  • If this pet does not work out, you must return it to our facility. If you return the pet within one week, you will receive a partial refund of your adoption fee. The pet may be returned at any time after one week but fees are not refunded. Vet records and tags must be returned or a $25 replacement fee will be charged.
  • Cats and kittens must be taken home in a plastic or cardboard carrier.
  • We do not adopt out dogs as guard dogs or "yard" dogs.
  • We do not release any animals to research facilities.
  • You must have the proper facilities to maintain and contain your pet before placement.
  • We do not adopt out animals as gifts.
  • All resident pets need to have current veterinary care and be spayed or neutered.
  • We ask that you take your new pet for a veterinary check within 5 working days.
  • A vet reference, vet check and/or a home visit may be required.


Foster Information

If you wish to temporarily provide a loving home for an animal in need, fostering is a great option. Fostering an animal is an immensely rewarding experience and changes animals' lives. Foster homes provide thousands of animals with the care and love they need in order to receive a second chance at life.

In order to qualify to foster animals, you must meet these certain qualifications:
  • You must have experience with animals
  • You must be able to provide a safe, stable, and loving environment
  • You must be able to transport foster animals to and from our shelter
  • You must have patience to deal with any animal illnesses and be willing to provide any necessary physical care for the animal
  • You must be at least 18 years old


Volunteer Information

We are always looking for people looking to volunteer and help around our shelter. As a volunteer, there are several opportunities available to you. Some of the opportunities are:

  • Fund Raising/ Special Events- Planning and implementing new and annual fund raising projects
  • Grounds and Maintenance- Help with flow beds, lawn mowing, painting, minor repairs and other projects to keep our facility in shape.
  • Kennel Help- We need volunteers that can come in the moring and evening to feed, water, clean and socialize
  • Office Work- Assist our office staff with various duties such as telephone duty, computer input, and much more
  • Saturday Volunteers- Volunteers need to work in the office on Saturday with duties including everything from answering the phne to socialilzing animals
  • Spay/Neuter- Volunteers to man the spay/neuter booth and also talk to animal owners and encourage spay/neutering
In order to qualify to be a volunteer, you must meet these certain qualifications:
  • You must have experience with animals- you have to have owned a pet or have worked with pets before
  • Minimum age requirement is 16 years
  • You must be physically and emotionally capable of caring for animals of all sizes
  • You must treat all animals in a humane, compassionate manner and ensure their safety at all times
  • Please contact us to apply
